SERVICE SETTINGS: Service Settings
 Revised by Mahesh Vasal
•
 about a year ago  •   1603 Views •  
Service Settings is used to configure many aspects of the service workflows, including
general setups, default cost centers, RO and Job flags, pre-invoicing rules, and many
others. This article defines all of the Service Settings.
PREREQUISITES:
You need the following permissions enabled to view and edit the Service Settings app
tile.
Service Setup
Access Service Settings
Edit Service Settings
Take me to the Permissions
Navigation to these permissions: Go to the Roles page > Permissions tab > Service drop-
down > RO section.
NAVIGATION: 
Take the following steps below to view and modify the Service Settings app tile:
Step 1. Open Service Settings app by clicking the App Grid menu > Settings tab >
Services Settings section > Service Settings app tile.
Take me to Service Settings
Navigate to the different sections within Service Settings to view or modify the settings
as needed. The following navigation options are available:

Round Up Settings 
Technician Actual

Round up Flag Hours to
nearest tenth of an hour
In service, actual, labor, bill, and flag hours can be entered to
the hundredth place or two numbers after the decimal. For
example, flag time can be entered as 1.56 hours. When toggled,
times can be set to round to the nearest tenth. A box displays
below the toggle to enter the value for the second decimal to
use for rounding. 
For example, if the toggle is set to round to the nearest tenth
with the second decimal greater than 5, the flag time of 1.56
hours will round to 1.60 hours. If the flag time is 1.53, the flag
time will round down to 1.50.

When toggled on, technicians can work on multiple ROs or

technicians can work on multiple jobs/ROs within those pay
types. For example, if technicians can only work on multiple
customer jobs, the first line for 'For Customer Pay Jobs' is
toggled on and the rest are off. If technicians work on multiple
customer pay and internal jobs, but only one warranty job at a
time, the 'For Customer - Internal Jobs' would be toggled to the
on position.

requirement for all
recommendations
When toggled on, the technicians are required to add a cause

recommended as a part of the inspection (MPI) process. The

The cause on jobs added to the repair order will not
require a cause statement.
Pre-Job Completion rules can be set in Service
Settings to require a cause for jobs on the repair order.
This setting requires a cause on recommendations
added through the MPI form.
Allow Creation of Custom
Concern Opcode
When toggled on, the option to 'Create' when entering an
opcode manually in the 'Add Job' or 'Add Recommendation'
workflows. The opcode is added and will be displayed on the
repair order. The opcode will not have the criteria needed to
dispatch for dealer partners with Auto Dispatch.
When toggled off, an opcode already defined in Opcode
Management must be selected when adding a job or
recommendation. This helps ensure opcodes are selected have
accurate details and pricing and will dispatch properly for
dealer partners with Auto Dispatch.
Service Module Selection:
Service Module Selection determines which job types are available within each workflow
when adding jobs. By default, all job types are available. In the Service Module Selection
table, the application, or workflow, in which the job type should be available is selected
from the dropdown. If a job type is not checked for a workflow, the option to add that job
type will not be available. 
Job types, including Return RO, Service Menu, Inspection, etc., can be set to
hide or show in the Add Job or Add Recommendations workflows in Appointments,
Repair Orders, Consumer Scheduling, Quotes, and Recommendations.
For example, the option to add a service menu from consumer scheduling can
be hidden but still display on a repair order.
The label for the job types that display when adding a job or recommendation
can be renamed in Keyword Configuration.
Take me to Keyword Configuration
Hold and drag the 6-dot kebab to change the order in which the job types
display when adding a job or recommendation.
Click Submit or refresh the page to save any changes.
Parts Request:
Service can send a request to parts automatically to bill parts to a repair order. For an
automatic parts fulfillment request to be sent, check the box next to the action from the
dropdown that will trigger the request. To automatically send a request at all trigger
points, check all three boxes.

Reminders:
Reminders can be sent to the service advisor when a customer's vehicle is approaching or
has past the promise time. For example, a reminder could be set to send a notification
every 15 minutes that the customer is still waiting. 
Enter the number of minutes prior to the promise time entered on the RO to
send the first reminder.
If desired, enter additional reminders.
To add additional reminders, enter the number of minutes in the open row. A
new row automatically displays each time a reminder is entered.
Click the trashcan icon to the right of the row to delete a reminder.
Express Mode Setup:
The Express mode service enables dealerships to quickly and efficiently complete
services in their express or quick lanes through the automatic fulfillment of parts for
opcodes done in Express mode. Once Express mode is enabled by Tekion support,
settings need to be configured that will determine whether the RO will be written in
Express mode or Regular mode.
Click here to learn more about Express Mode
Tags:
Repair Orders can have RO Level Tags attached for filtering within the RO List and, in
some cases, to report to the OEM through integration. For example, if an RO is created for
the Body Shop, an RO Tag displays on the RO with the label of Body Shop.
**Note: The RO tags configured here are different from the tag number on the repair order.
RO tags will automatically appear on the RO List based on the filters set.
Standard tags will be included by default and cannot be renamed. The standard tags are:
Main Shop
Body Shop
Mobile Shop
Express Shop
New Vehicle Shop (for PDI)
Used Vehicle Shop (for UVI)
The standard RO tags are sent through OEM integrations for Mercedes-Benz and
Toyota based on their requirements.
Custom tags are displayed on the RO List and can be used as a filter within the
RO List.
A repair order can have multiple RO tags depending on the filters used to define
the RO Tag.
Click and drag the 6-dot kebab to indicate the order in which the RO tags should
be displayed on the RO List. The first RO tag will display on the RO List. If more than
one RO Tag is assigned to a repair order, a plus sign with the number of additional
RO tags displays. Click the number to view the additional RO tags.
Click the icon in the Archive column to remove the tag from the Active Tags list.
The tag will move to the Archived Tags tab.
Click the Archived Tags tab to move an archived tag back to active.
Job Tags:
Job tags allow you to define color-coded job tags that are seen on the RO and PDF
screens. You can define both the flag color, as well as the text. Checking the boxes - RO
and PDF - associated with the tag will change the visibility for those tags.
RO Flag:
RO Flags allow you to define color-coded flags used to convey information about the RO
or its status. RO flags and their corresponding color are defined by the dealership. The RO
flag can be selected under the RO Flag Column for the corresponding RO on the RO List
and the RO Dashboard when the RO Dashboard has been enabled by support. The RO
Flags are manually applied to the repair order.
**Note: A white flag should be set up when using RO Flags to designate "No Flag." Once an
RO Flag is selected on an RO, the flag can only be changed. There is no option to remove the
flag. The flag can be changed to white indicating there are no flags on the RO.
Hold:
Users with permission to 'View and Edit Service Settings' can view and edit Hold Types.
Hold Types can be added, removed, or renamed and any modifications made to hold
types will be effective immediately. In cases where there are ROs associated with a hold
type that is being removed, users will have the option of either removing the holds placed
on those ROs ('un-hold') or replacing the hold type being removed with a different one.
The following Hold Types are available by default: Hold for Parts, Hold for Authorization,
Hold for Diagnostic, and Hold for Sublet. Any of these can be modified or removed. The
following Hold Type is available by default: Hold for Parts, Hold for Authorization, Hold for
Diagnostic, and Hold for Sublet, and cannot be modified or removed.

RO KPI List:
The metrics seen on the top of the RO list table can be configured here. To create a new
KPI, enter the desired criteria using the filter funnel, select the role(s) to view the KPI, and
select whether the role can view the KPI for all ROs, the team's ROs, or ROs specific to the
user.
**Note: This setting works in tandem with the permissions 'Show all Repair Orders in list'
and 'Show only Team Repair Orders in list' under Service in the Roles app. 
For example, if 'My Team' is selected under the 'Specific To' column, but permission is not
granted to 'Show all Repair Orders in list' or 'Show only Team Repair Orders in list,' the user
will see the numbers for the team on the RO List KPI, but will not be able to click the KPI to
use as a filter on the RO List. The filter will be viewed only.
Take me to Permissions page
For Total Sales, select the pay type, the components of the repair order to include the
total sales, the role(s) that can view the KPIs, and whether the role can view the KPI for all
ROs, the team's ROs, or ROs specific to the user. 
Quotes:
The Quotes section has the following settings: 
Quote Flags - Quote flags are color-coded indicators to convey information
about the Quote or its status when viewing the quote from the main list within the
Quotes application or viewing the details of an individual quote.
Quote flags are defined at the dealership level. The service department
defines the flag color and what that color represents.
A color legend appears when clicking the quote flag in the Quotes app.  
Take me to Quotes page
Set quotes to expire after - Quotes can be set to expire after the number of
days, months, or years designated in the 'Set quotes to expire after' prompt. This
allows a quote to be honored for a set period and protects profit as prices fluctuate.
Quotes that have not been converted to an appointment or an RO by the
number of days set will automatically be set to expired status.
Expired quotes can only be cloned or voided. The quote is not
automatically set to a void status to retain the ability to clone and create a
new quote without duplicate entry.
The expiration date can be set to print on Quote PDFs through the PDF
Configurator in PDF Settings.
Take me to PDF Settings
Role Configuration:
The role configuration section is used to change the Vehicle Profile layout based on the
role. The Vehicle Profile section is used to change access to specific sections of the vehicle
profiles. The Roles section defines which roles are able to access the selected tabs within
the vehicle profile.

Approval Settings:
The Approval Settings determine whether warranty jobs and recommendations need
approval to be added and whether additional time beyond the allowed warranty time for
a job is needed to complete warranty work. Approval settings are often needed for
compliance with warranty audits. When toggled on, the RO Approval workflow is enabled.

Description

Deferred Recommendation Rules: 
The Deferred Recommendation Rules determine how long a deferred recommendation
will be displayed on the repair order and within the customer's vehicle profile. 
Within the vehicle profile, all deferred services will be displayed with the
deferring customer listed. The customer deferring the recommendation will be
listed in the table.
When adding a recommendation to a repair order, within the check-in process,
and on the consumer portal, only the recommendations deferred by the current
customer and within the time period established here will be displayed.
Job Clock Setup:
The Job Clock Setup determines whether a job clock reason is applicable and whether a
job clock reason is required when a technician clocks in and out of work on a job. 
The following sections are present:
Section
Description
Job Click In/Out
Reason Settings

The following selections are available when a technician clocks in or out of a
job:
Applicable:
When checked, the reason box displays under the hour
dropdown. The technician can enter a reason for clocking in or
out of a job. For example, a technician could enter 'Running
straight time' when selecting 'Other' from the hour description
dropdown above the reason.
When unchecked, the reason box is not displayed.
Mandatory:
When checked, a reason is required. A reason must be
entered to clock in or clock out. If a reason is not entered, the
reason box is outlined in red and the technician cannot proceed
with the clock-in or clock-out until a reason is entered.
When unchecked, the reason box is available to enter a
reason for running time however, the entry is not required.

is marked to require clock-in in Opcode Management.

Profit and Loss View Setup:
Copy KB URL
If desired, configure the range to designate when the gross profit reflects a decrease or an
increase in the Service Settings. By default, gross profit amounts below zero will display a
red arrow. Gross profit amounts above zero will display a green arrow. A gross profit of
zero or in the middle of a range defined in Service Settings will display a black arrow.
**Note: The Profit and Loss View is only available to users logged in with either the Service
Manager or Service Advisor role and permission to Profit/Loss View.
The following fields display:
Field
Description
Checkbox
Check the box to enable additional ranges. The range is used
to designate when the gross profit reflects a decrease or an
increase in the Service Settings based on the service
department's goal. By default, gross profit amounts below
zero will display a red arrow. Gross profit amounts above
zero will display a green arrow. A gross profit of zero or in the
middle of a range defined in Service Settings will display a
black arrow.
Mapped Symbol
The mapped symbol indicates the arrow color that will
display based on the range entered.
P/L % Value
Less Than - Enter the gross profit percentage to which a red
arrow should display for a gross profit percentage below the
entered value.
More Than / Equal To - Enter the gross profit percentage to

Gross profit percentages between the range will display a
black arrow in the Profit and Loss View within the repair
